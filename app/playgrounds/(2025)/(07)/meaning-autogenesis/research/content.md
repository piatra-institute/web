# Meaning Autogenesis: Can Self-Repair Be the Seed of Meaning?

## Abstract

This playground dramatizes one of the boldest ideas in theoretical biology: that
the simplest form of meaning might emerge from a chemical system that maintains
itself. It builds Terrence Deacon's "autogen", a self-bounding, self-repairing
structure made of an autocatalytic set, and frames its repair response in the
language of Peirce's semiotics. The science underneath is an autocatalytic
reaction set; the claim on top, that this is proto-meaning, is a philosophical
interpretation, and the playground keeps the two apart.

## The autocatalytic core

The molecules react by a small fixed table. A C catalyst converts an A substrate
into another C; an F catalyst converts a D substrate into a G. The crucial
property is that C makes more C: the catalyst is regenerated, not consumed. A set
of reactions that collectively produces its own catalysts is an autocatalytic
set, the standard formal candidate for a self-propagating chemistry. The
calibration panel checks this table directly: A plus C yields a C, D plus F yields
a G, and the reaction rule switches off beyond a set level.

## From autocatalysis to autogen

Deacon's autogen adds a second ingredient: containment. The catalysts also build
a capsule (here drawn as a growing capsule that encloses C and F molecules). Now
the system does two things at once, it reproduces its catalysts and it bounds
itself, and the two processes are mutually dependent. Disrupt the capsule and the
released catalysts rebuild it. That coupling of self-production and
self-containment is what makes the autogen more than a puddle of reactions: it has
something to maintain.

## The semiotic reading

Here is the interpretive leap. When the capsule is broken, the system responds
the same way to every disruption: rebuild. Deacon, following Peirce, reads this
uniform response as a minimal act of interpretation, an iconic interpretant, where
a disruption is a sign and the rebuild is the system's "reading" of it. On this
view, the most primitive form of meaning is not representation but the
self-preserving response of a system that has a self to preserve.

This is a genuinely contested philosophical claim (Deacon's teleodynamics), not a
result the simulation proves. The dynamics show self-repair; calling it meaning is
an interpretation, and the assumptions panel marks it as speculative.

## What is exact, and what is not

The reaction table and the distance metric are deterministic and exact, and the
calibration pins them. Everything else, the spatial motion, the encounters, the
capsule formation, is a stochastic 2D particle caricature with no real kinetics.
And the framing as meaning is philosophy layered on the mechanics. The playground
is a way to think about how self-maintenance could bootstrap something
meaning-like, not evidence that it does.
